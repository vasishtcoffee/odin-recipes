Description

Pav Bhaji is one of Mumbai's most iconic street foods, loved for its spicy, buttery, and flavorful taste. It's a delicious blend of mashed vegetables cooked with a special mix of spices, topped with a dollop of butter, and served hot with soft pav (bread rolls) toasted in butter.

Ingredients~

FOR BHAJI

  • 2 tbsp butter (plus more for serving)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped or pureed
  • 1 capsicum (green bell pepper), finely chopped
  • 2 medium potatoes, boiled & mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower, boiled & mashed
  • 1 cup green peas, boiled
  • 1 carrot, boiled & mashed (optional)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (optional for spice)
  • 1-2 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • Â½ tsp red chili powder
  • Â½ tsp turmeric powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Â½ lemon (for juice)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (for garnish)

FOR PAV

  • 8 pav buns (soft bread rolls)
  • 2-3 tbsp butter (to toast)

STEPS

  1. Boil potatoes, cauliflower, peas, and carrot until soft. Mash them lightly and keep aside.
  2. Heat butter and oil in a large pan.
  3. Add finely chopped onions and sautÃ© until golden brown.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
  5. Add capsicum and cook for 2-3 minutes.
  6. Mix in pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric, and salt. Cook until oil separates.
  7. Add the boiled and mashed vegetables into the pan.
  8. Pour 1-1.5 cups of water and mash everything together until smooth.
  9. Simmer the bhaji on low flame for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  10. Add lemon juice and garnish with coriander leaves.
  11. Slice pav buns and toast them with butter on a hot pan until golden.
  12. Serve the hot bhaji with butter-toasted pav, chopped onions, and lemon wedges.
